# Alpha memo: senescent cell clearance and tissue repair tradeoffs in longevity interventions

## Core signal
Two pilot studies of the identical senolytic regimen (dasatinib 100 mg/d + quercetin 1250 mg/d, 3 days/week × 3 weeks) in idiopathic pulmonary fibrosis (IPF) report divergent tolerability signal directions despite a shared population, dose, and schedule. Receipt 1 (open-label, n=14) was interpreted by its authors as a feasibility-positive pilot. Receipt 2 (placebo-controlled, NCT02874989, n=12) reframes that feasibility — non-serious adverse events were roughly 3× higher in the D+Q arm (65 vs 22), and sleep/anxiety clustered disproportionately in the D+Q arm (4/6 vs 0/6). Same drug, same organ, same week-3 endpoint, different conclusion.

## The 2+2=5 angle
The bridge is the *label*, not the *measure*. Receipt 1's "feasibility" was operationalized as retention + completion — both 100% in both trials. That metric is silent on symptom burden. Once a placebo arm is introduced (Receipt 2), the same AEs are re-read as a tolerability tax. The 2+2=5 is the additive effect of (a) using retention as a proxy for tolerability, (b) an open-label design inflating patient-reported outcomes, and (c) absence of a control, which together let a non-trivial AE profile (sleep, anxiety, GI, respiratory, skin) read as a clean feasibility pilot rather than a tolerability question.

## Why this could matter
For longevity-channel positioning of senolytics, the unit of evidence investors and consumers see is the *pilot* label, not the AE table. If a regulator, KOL, or consumer brand is selecting on "pilot completed" vs. "tolerability burden," Receipt 1 vs Receipt 2 show how the same receipts invert. Cross-domain transfer: this is a general warning for single-arm open-label pilot readouts in senolytic IPF and adjacent senescent-cell-clearance programs, where retention/completion metrics can mask disproportionate symptomatic load until a controlled pilot is run.

## What would break the idea
- A larger, powered RCT in IPF showing AE parity with placebo at the same D+Q dose (would null the boundary condition).
- Re-analyses of Receipt 1 AEs showing a matched severity profile to Receipt 2 (would mean the two trials are consistent, not contradictory).
- Demonstration that the sleep/anxiety signal is mechanism-driven (off-target kinase effects of D) rather than symptomatic/expectancy — would make the label-vs-measure bridge irrelevant.

## Receipts
- 10.1016/j.ebiom.2018.12.052 — open-label, single-arm, two-center pilot, n=14 IPF, D+Q × 3 weeks.
- 10.1016/j.ebiom.2023.104481 — phase I, single-blind, placebo-controlled pilot, NCT02874989, n=12 IPF, same regimen.

## Safety note
Receipt 1 reported one serious adverse event (relationship unspecified in abstract). Receipt 2 reported no serious adverse events related to D+Q. Both trials describe primarily mild–moderate non-serious events; respiratory, skin/bruising, GI, and sleep/anxiety symptoms predominated. Generalization beyond the IPF pilot population is not supported by these receipts.
